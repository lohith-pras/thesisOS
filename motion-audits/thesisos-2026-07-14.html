ThesisOS — workflow motion is mostly quiet, with two noisy seams.

A local-first thesis research workspace and marketing page built with vanilla HTML, CSS, and DOM rendering. The audit weights productivity restraint first, then production polish; delight remains selective for rare onboarding only.

Overall

The interface’s motion posture is appropriately restrained for serious, repeat-use research work. Buttons are crisp and reduced motion is broadly respected. The only conspicuous motion is concentrated around long-running work, where the app duplicates a nine-dot loop and removes modals without a closing state.

Emil Kowalski — Restraint & Speed

Primary

Concern: the frequent action layer is excellent; the waiting layer is more animated than necessary.

What’s working well
  • app/styles.css and landing/styles.css use a shared custom Bézier and 160–180ms button feedback, including a restrained press scale.
  • Navigation and page swaps are immediate. That is the correct decision for a keyboard- and task-heavy workspace.
  • Hover lift is gated to fine pointers, avoiding touch-device hover artifacts.
Issues to address
ImportantEmilPerformance

Nine-dot activity loop is duplicated during active work

WhatactivityStrip() and sectionActivity() each create nine indefinitely animating dots. A workflow view can show both at once: eighteen independently delayed scale/opacity animations.

Why it mattersIt reads as a decorative “thinking” effect rather than progress, competes with the status copy, and remains visible while actions such as drafting or retrieval take meaningful time. The reduced-motion fallback prevents harm, but the default is still unnecessarily busy for this context.

Recommended motionKeep one status location. Enter it once with opacity + translateY(-4px) over 180ms, then use a static marker and changing text. If indeterminate progress must be signalled, use one compact spinner only—not a staggered grid.

Opportunities
  • Do not animate view changes or task-row selection. These are frequent, goal-directed interactions and the existing instant swap is correct.
  • When a workflow has measurable stages, update the existing status text rather than adding a visual progress choreography.
Through Emil’s lens: ThesisOS has already made the hard, correct call: it does not animate its everyday work surface. Carry that same restraint into the in-progress state.

Jakub Krehel — Production Polish

Secondary

Concern: entering task detail is clear, but closing it lacks the gentler counterpart.

What’s working well
  • The task modal enters with opacity, a small 8px translation, and a conservative .98 scale; it avoids the jarring scale-from-zero pattern.
  • Reduced-motion rules disable animation and smooth scrolling globally in both frontend stylesheets.
  • Animated properties are transform and opacity, not layout properties; no broad will-change declarations were found.
Issues to address
ImportantJakubState transition

Task modal is removed before it can exit

WhatThe modal gets only modal-in; its close control and several action paths call .remove() immediately.

Why it mattersThe user sees a composed panel materialize but snap away. This is particularly noticeable after approving or rejecting a task—the exact moment the app should confirm a deliberate state change without introducing delay.

Recommended motionAdd a closing class, wait for a 140ms transition, then remove the node. Keep the exit quieter than entry: opacity to 0, translateY(-3px), scale(.99). Preserve the current immediate removal under prefers-reduced-motion.

Opportunities
  • Add a 150–180ms color transition to navigation items and text buttons. They currently snap on hover while primary buttons are polished.
  • Keep the landing page static beyond its button feedback. Its molecule illustration already provides enough visual richness without an ambient loop.
Through Jakub’s lens: The app benefits from motion when it maintains continuity at a meaningful boundary. Modal close is one of those boundaries; the rest should remain almost invisible.

Jhey Tompkins — Experimentation & Delight

Selective

Strong: the product earns its calm through concrete visual character rather than attention-seeking animation.

What’s working well
  • The landing page’s CSS molecule illustration offers a memorable, domain-relevant visual without parallax, spinning, or a looping hero animation.
  • No gradient-text, hover-scale-everything, or staggered entrance patterns were found.
Opportunities
  • For a first-run-only onboarding success state, a single 180–240ms check-icon swap could provide warmth. Do not repeat it on routine saves.
Through Jhey’s lens: Reserve experimentation for rare first-use moments. The product’s distinctive idea is traceability, not spectacle; its visual illustration already carries the delight budget.
